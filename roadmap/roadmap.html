  • Actionable Takeaways in Reflections

    In your project reflections, it would be beneficial to include clear and actionable takeaways. Think about what you learned from the process and how you would approach similar challenges differently in the future. These reflections give insight into your growth as a designer and your ability to apply lessons to future projects. Employers want to know not only what you did, but what you have learned from each experience.

  • You might need to consider using stronger visual styles

    One suggestion for improving your portfolio is to simplify the layout and avoid overwhelming your audience with too much information at once. Remember, recruiters often skim through portfolios quickly. Try to make your portfolio structure clear, intuitive, and focused. Each project should be easy to navigate, with brief descriptions upfront and more detailed breakdowns as they scroll. Highlight key outcomes and learnings from each project to keep viewers engaged without overwhelming them.
